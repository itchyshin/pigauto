4. Key Findings & Decision Points

What works

  • Continuous & count RMSE (simulated): GNN consistently improves over BM by 0.7–2.5% across 13 of 14 comparable scenarios. Best gains under variable phylogenetic signal (S13: +2.5%) and larger trees (S14: +2.0%).
  • Gate mechanism for discrete traits: Binary, categorical, and ordinal accuracy/RMSE are exactly identical between methods in every scenario. The learnable gates correctly defer to the BM baseline, guaranteeing zero degradation.
  • AVONET coverage: 95% prediction intervals are well-calibrated on real data (90–96%), which is a key selling point for imputation under uncertainty.
  • Multi-obs architecture: S15 demonstrates the aggregate→message-pass→expand pipeline works (r = 0.70 for continuous, 61.7% accuracy for binary with 3 obs/species).

What needs work

  • AVONET continuous RMSE: GNN is 0.8–12.1% worse than BM on real morphometric data, despite near-identical Pearson r. The rank ordering is correct but the scale is inflated. This is the primary issue to address.
  • S15 coverage at 8.7%: The multi-obs uncertainty quantification is broken — prediction intervals are far too narrow. The SE expansion from species-level baseline to obs-level may not account for within-species variance.
  • S11 regime shift: The only scenario where GNN is worse (−0.6%). The bimodal structure may confuse the DAE’s noise model. Low priority but worth investigating.
  • Simulation coverage generally below 95%: Most scenarios show 84–93% coverage (nominal = 95%). The SE estimates are systematically too tight.
  • Discrete trait gates are too conservative: Accuracy is identical in every case, meaning the GNN never improves on BM for discrete traits. The gates may be shutting too aggressively.

Suggested priorities for next phase

  • Priority 1: Fix AVONET RMSE regression. Likely causes: (a) residual scale not matching BM predictions, (b) learning rate or epoch tuning, (c) the GNN adding noise rather than signal when BM is already strong. Consider a weighted ensemble or residual shrinkage.
  • Priority 2: Fix multi-obs coverage (S15). The SE computation needs to incorporate within-species variance, not just phylogenetic uncertainty.
  • Priority 3: Improve general coverage calibration. Options: conformal prediction wrapper, or scaling the SE by an empirically estimated factor from the validation set.
  • Priority 4: Allow discrete-trait gates to partially open when phylogenetic structure carries information beyond the mode/majority class.
  • Priority 5: Add scenario S16 (interaction terms) if it didn’t complete, and benchmark against additional real datasets beyond AVONET.
